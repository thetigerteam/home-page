Intentions of the project

Our project has the aim of creating the space station which will substitute the ISS once its mission will be at an end. We have considered some key elements that are going to be protagonist in the long-term space exploration and we have applied them to the new space station so they’ll be tested. In this way the new international space station is going to be the outpost of humankind like its predecessor.




The Space Station is a really bright example of how international differences move completely to the background when there is a greater purpose, a common passion.

The ODYSSEUS II Project has received funding from the European Union's Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme. It is funded with nearly two million Euros by the European Commission in the framework of EU HORIZON 2020, started on 1st January 2015 and will run until the 31st December 2017. A strong consortium of fourteen partners and four supporting organizations from eleven European countries will collaborate to provide learning opportunities in space science and technology to European youth. Italian partners are Politecnico di Torino and Altec.
